Bathless Cheesecake

Home

Description

I love cheesecake as do most, and I remember the very first time I baked one. The trickiest and most inconvenient part was the bath. Trying to find the right size and depth of pan to achieve this apparently crucial step was more headache than it was worth (to me at least). I finsihed that cheescake in my newly bought springform pan, cleaned it, and banished it in my cupboard for years.

Fast forward about six years and I found myself promising a cheesecake for an engagement party, I was dreading it. I was haunted by the pressure and headache of the bath. So I started searching for a way to get around it. Thank God others felt my pain, for there were recipes that existed! This recipe that I found has been my go to since. The only tweaks were to the crust, as I prefer a thicker and higher one. I have done strawberry topping and even a pumpkin filling with this recipe, so feel free to add your own toppings or fillings to this base! I hope you enjoy this simpler recipe as much as I do!

Note: It is very important that certain ingredients are ROOM TEMP, otherwise the consistency of the cheesecake will be all wrong!

Ingredients

Graham Cracker Crust

  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs (~2 sleeves worth)
  • 2 Tbs sugar
  • 1 1/2 Tbs brown sugar
  • 10 Tbs butter (melted)

Cheesecake

  • 32 oz cream cheese (ROOM TEMP)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2/3 cup sour cream (ROOM TEMP)
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 4 large eggs (ROOM TEMP)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 325F
  2. Combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and brown sugar.
  3. Add melted butter and use a fork to combine well.
  4. Pour crumbs into 9" springform pan and press firmly into the bottom and up the sides. Set aside.
  5. In a bowl (or stand mixer), add cream cheese and stir until smooth and creamy. Don't overbeat or you'll incorporate too much air (consistency is key!).
  6. Add sugar and stir until creamy.
  7. Add sour cream, vanilla extract, and salt. Stir until well combined.
  8. Lightly beat the eggs seperately and gradually add them (~1 egg worth at a time), mixing until each egg is just incorporated.
  9. Pour cheesecake batter into the prepared springform pan. Shimmy the pan a bit to even the mixture and allow any air to escape. Place on a baking sheet that's been lined with foil (to insure against leaks).
  10. Transfer to the center rack of your oven and bake on 325F for 50-60 minutes (or longer as needed). Edges will likely be slightly puffed and may have just begun to turn a light golden brown and the center should spring back to the touch but still be "Jello-jiggly".
  11. Remove from oven and allow to cool on top of the oven (so it cools most gradually) for 10 minutes. Use a knife to gently loosen the crust from the springform pan on the edges (helps prevent cracks as the cheesecake cools and shrinks). Do not remove or loosen the springform pan!
  12. Allow cheesecake to cool another 1-2 hours or until near room temp before transferring to refrigerator. Cool overnight or for at least 6 hours.
  13. Remove from springform pan and ENJOY!
